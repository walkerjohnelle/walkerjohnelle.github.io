Open Source Software in Defense

Prior to attending Skill Distillery™ I've spent the past few months interning for Silicon Mountain Technologies in Lakewood, CO. While working there, I had the chance to work on some interesting projects, including providing my experience and insight on a few projects. One of which was writing a blog post for the company's website about the use of open source software in the defense industry. Here's a brief summary and a link to said post.

In the realm of national defense, staying ahead of emerging threats is not only a priority, but a necessity. To achieve this, the Department of Defense (DoD) has been embracing open source software (OSS) as a powerful tool in its arsenal. OSS, with its transparent and collaborative nature, has become an essential asset in enabling the DoD to tackle challenges effectively, innovate rapidly, and enhance its capabilities while maintaining cost-efficiency. Favorite Movies from 2023:

Below is a list of my favorite movies from 2023, so far. Here, we have everything from an animated superhero film, to a couple of historical dramas, along with a surreal horror comedy, and a unbelievable documentary. I've linked the trailers just in case you're interested.

  • Spider-Man (Across the Spider-Verse)

    So I enjoyed the first one, however, the second installment of the Spider-Verse series might of just ruined superhero movies for me for awhile. A lot of movies now are delving into the whole multiverse thing, but to me, this one did it best. Merging fan service with fresh storytelling, and incredible visuals, it sets the bar extremely high for telling old stories with a fresh take.

  • Killers of the Flower Moon

    The trailer really threw me off. Initially, I saw the trailer and thought, sure that looks interesting enough, but when i heard it was 3.5 hours long, there was definitely a pause for concern. I barely got through Martin Scorsese's "The Irishman," and I was worried this would be another drawn out movie that would struggle to hold my interest. What I got was a very complex telling of a story that has repetedly been swept under the rug and that really needed to be told. I walked out feeling a wave of emotions and it's definitely a film that has stayed with me days after leaving the cinema.

  • Beau is Afraid

    I love horror movies, especially ones written and directed by Ari Aster. So i was really excited to see this one. Another film with a 3 hour runtime on this list, the first watch was a lot to take in. I enjoyed it, and it definitely stuck with me, but it didn't make this list until i watched it again. There were so many nuances that I missed the first time or just didn't fully appreciate. It's definitely not for everyone, but for the people that will enjoy it, I think they'll love it!

  • Oppenheimer

    Yet another 3 hour movie, I swear this isn't intentional! This movie needs no explination. The marketing campaign behind Barbenheimer ensured that there would be plenty of eyes on this one. It's a story that I've always been interested in, and I feel like it was told & acted extremely well. Despite the runtime, I felt like it used it's time very efficiently and I didn't even notice.

  • BS High

    I stumbled on this documentary one night as I struggled to find something to watch. I watched the trailer and figured I'd give it a shot. I was not ready for the ride I went on. I couldn't believe the lengths people would go to make a buck and the justifications people will make to validate their horrible actions.
